Displaying LaTeX Math using MathJax

Inline Math

When you have an equation like $E = mc^2$ inline with text, you use single dollar signs. Another famous inline example is Euler's identity: $e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$. You can also use parentheses delimiters: \( a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \).

Display Math (Block Level)

For equations that should stand on their own line, use double dollar signs:

$$ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6} $$

Or you can use square bracket delimiters:

\[ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} dx = \sqrt{\pi} \]

Here's the quadratic formula:

$$ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} $$
