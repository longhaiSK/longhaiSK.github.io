Important Note:

Students are expected to have a strong background in the probability theory, which would be gained by taking a course such as STAT 342. Although not strictly required, it would also be useful for students to have had some exposure to applied statistical methods, to provide context and intuition for the models that will be discussed. If you are interested in taking the course, and do not have these prerequisites, you should be prepared to convince the instructor that you have sufficient equivalent background to succeed in the course.

Course Description

This course discusses the theoretical foundations for making inferences about probabilistic models from data. Consideration will be given to Bayesian, likelihood, and frequentist paradigms.

Learning Objectives

After completing this course, students are expected to

  • be able to understand the Bayesian, and frequentist paradigms for statistical inferences, and therefore develop a high-level understanding of various applied statistical methods

  • be able to apply the theoretical results in statistical inference to practical statistical problems

  • be able to evaluate different statistical methods with appropriate criteria

  • gain a comparative appreciation of different inferential paradigms

Term Tests and Final Examination:

Both the term test and the final exam will be closed book. Exam questions will require students to demonstrate understanding of concepts and definitions, and to state and possibly prove theorems presented in class. In addition, students will be required to solve problems similar to those done during lab periods. The mid-term exam will be one hour in length. The final exam will be three hours.
